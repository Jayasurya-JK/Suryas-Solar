## **1. Monocrystalline Solar Panels (Mono Panels)**

**The highest efficiency and best performance**

Monocrystalline panels are made from a single, pure silicon crystal, making them the most advanced and efficient type of solar panel currently available.

**Key Benefits:**

* High efficiency: **18%‚Äì22%**
* Performs better in **low-light and high-temperature** conditions
* Requires less rooftop space
* Sleek black appearance

**Best For:**\
Homes with **limited roof area**, premium installations, customers seeking maximum output and long-term savings.

## **2. Polycrystalline Solar Panels (Poly Panels)**

**Affordable and reliable for larger rooftops**

Polycrystalline panels are manufactured from multiple silicon crystals, offering a balance between performance and cost.

**Key Benefits:**

* Efficiency: **15%‚Äì17%**
* Lower cost compared to mono panels
* Great option for large residential and commercial rooftops
* Distinct blue-textured appearance

**Best For:**\
Budget-friendly installations, homes with **ample rooftop space**, small businesses, and farmhouses.

<!--EndFragment-->



<!--StartFragment-->

## **3. Thin-Film Solar Panels**

**Lightweight and ideal for industrial-scale projects**

Thin-Film panels are produced by depositing photovoltaic material onto glass or metal surfaces. They are less common in residential systems but useful for specific commercial applications.

**Key Benefits:**

* Lightweight and flexible
* Performs consistently in cloudy or shaded areas
* Easy installation for large surface coverage

**Best For:**\
Warehouses, commercial sheds, factories, and projects requiring **large area coverage** with lower structural load.
