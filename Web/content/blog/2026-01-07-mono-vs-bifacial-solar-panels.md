Salesmen will tell you it generates 30% more power. They will say it works at night (spoiler: it doesn't). They will say it's indestructible.

##  The Technology: One Face vs. Two Faces

### 1. Monocrystalline (Mono-PERC)
*   **Design:** Generates power only from the front (Sun facing). The backsheet is opaque (White or Black plastic).
*   **Efficiency:** ~21-22%.
*   **The Status Quo:** This is the reliable workhorse. 80% of homes in India still use this because it is cheaper and proven.

### 2. Bifacial (Glass-Glass)
*   **Design:** Generates power from **Both Sides**.
    *   **Front:** Captures direct sunlight (same as Mono).
    *   **Back:** Captures **Albedo Light** (light reflecting off the ground/roof).
*   **Construction:** Instead of a plastic backsheet, it has a second layer of glass.
*   **Efficiency:** ~22% Front + **5% to 25% Backside Gain**.

---

##  The "White Roof" Factor: The Cuddalore Secret

Bifacial panels rely entirely on **Albedo** (Reflectivity). If you put a Bifacial panel on a dark surface, the backside generates nothing.

**Reflectivity Index of Cuddalore Surfaces:**
*   **Black Tar/Bitumen:** 0% Reflection (Bifacial is useless).
*   **Green Grass/Mat:** 10% Reflection (Minimal gain).
*   **Red Clay Tiles:** 15% Reflection (Small gain).
*   **Grey Concrete:** 25% Reflection (Decent gain).
*   **White Cool Roof Paint:** **70% Reflection (Massive Gain).**

**The Cuddalore Advantage:**
Most terraces in Cuddalore are painted with White Cool Roof Paint to beat the summer heat.
**This is the secret sauce.** On a white terrace in Cuddalore, a Bifacial panel acts like a super-charger. The white floor acts like a mirror, bouncing massive amounts of light onto the rear face.

> **Field Test Data:** In a installation in Manjakuppam, a 5kW Bifacial plant on a white roof generated **28 Units/day**, compared to 23 Units/day from a standard Mono plant nearby. That is a **21% boost.**

---

##  Durability: The Glass-Glass Advantage

It's not just about power; it's about life.
Standard panels use a plastic backsheet. In Cuddalore's UV-intense sun, plastic degrades, turns yellow, and eventually cracks over 15 years.

Bifacial panels are **Dual Glass**.
*   **Zero Plastic:** Glass doesn't yellow.
*   **Zero Micro-Cracks:** The "sandwich" structure is much stiffer. When wind shakes the panel, the solar cells inside don't bend or crack.
*   **Fire Rating:** Glass doesn't burn. It is safer.
*   **Warranty:** Most Bifacial panels come with a **30 Year Performance Warranty** (vs 25 years for Mono).

##  The Verdict: Which one should YOU buy?

**Scenario A: "I have limited roof space."**
**Winner: Bifacial.** You need to squeeze every watt out of a small area. The extra generation justifies the cost because expanding the system later is hard.

**Scenario B: "I have a large, white-painted terrace."**
**Winner: Bifacial.** The reflection bonus makes this a no-brainer. You will get 5kW performance from a 4kW plant.

**Scenario C: "I have a red tiled/sloped roof or low budget."**
**Winner: Mono PERC.** Without a reflective floor or height, Bifacial is a waste of money. Stick to the proven Mono panels—they are still excellent technology.
