## 1. What Determines the Cost of a Solar System?

A residential rooftop solar system is not a single product; it is an engineered system made up of multiple critical components. The price of each component varies significantly based on quality tier, brand reputation, and warranty terms.

The four main cost drivers are:

### A. Solar Panels (Modules)
This is the most critical long-term investment. Solar panels directly determine how much electricity you generate for the next 25 years. Cheap panels degrade faster, have lower efficiency, and are more likely to develop internal "hot spots" that reduce output dramatically over time.

Panel types available in Tamil Nadu:
*   **Budget Tier (Locally assembled, Indian B-grade cells):** ₹18 to ₹22 per Watt
*   **Mid-Range (Tier-2 brands, genuine A-grade cells):** ₹22 to ₹28 per Watt
*   **Premium Tier-1 (Waaree, Adani, Tata, Premier Energies – genuine Tier-1 cells):** ₹28 to ₹38 per Watt

At Surya's Solar, we exclusively use Tier-1 manufacturer panels. The performance warranty guarantee means that after 25 years, your panels will still produce at least 80% of their original rated output.

### B. Solar Inverter
The inverter converts the DC power from your panels into the 230V AC that your home appliances use. It is the brain of the system. 

*   **Budget Single-Phase String Inverter (Chinese no-brand):** ₹8,000 to ₹15,000
*   **Quality Single-Phase String Inverter (Growatt, Solis, Deye, Havells):** ₹18,000 to ₹35,000
*   **Premium String Inverter (SMA, Fronius, SolarEdge):** ₹40,000 to ₹80,000

A good inverter should carry a minimum 5-year manufacturer warranty. The inverter is the component most likely to require replacement during the 25-year system life.

### C. Mounting Structure
The structure holds your panels to the roof. In coastal Tamil Nadu (Cuddalore, Puducherry), the structure must be manufactured from **hot-dip galvanized steel or marine-grade aluminum** to withstand the highly corrosive salt-laden air environment. Cheap untreated iron structures will begin to rust within 2 to 3 years in coastal areas.

*   **Budget (untreated mild steel):** ₹5,000 to ₹8,000 for 3kW
*   **Quality (hot-dip galvanized):** ₹12,000 to ₹18,000 for 3kW
*   **Premium (marine-grade aluminum):** ₹20,000 to ₹30,000 for 3kW

### D. BOS (Balance of System): Cables, Connectors, Junction Boxes, DCDB, ACDB
This includes all the solar-grade DC cables, MC4 connectors, DC Distribution Boxes (DCDB), AC Distribution Boards (ACDB), earthing kits, and lightning arrestors. Cheap standard electrical cables used instead of UV-resistant solar cables will degrade rapidly under the harsh Indian sun and can become a fire hazard.

*   **Quality BOS components for a 3kW system:** ₹8,000 to ₹15,000

## 3. The PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana: Subsidy Details (2026)

The central government's PM Surya Ghar scheme provides a massive direct financial subsidy to residential homeowners installing On-Grid rooftop solar. The subsidy is transferred directly to the homeowner's bank account within 30 to 90 days of commissioning.

### Subsidy Amounts (Central Government Component):
*   **For 1kW system:** ₹30,000
*   **For 2kW system:** ₹60,000
*   **For 3kW and above:** ₹78,000 (maximum central subsidy)

**Eligibility Requirements:**
1. You must be a residential homeowner (not tenant, not commercial entity).
2. The installation must be done through an MNRE-registered PM Surya Ghar empanelled vendor.
3. Your property must have a valid electricity connection in your name.
4. You must apply through the official PM Surya Ghar portal (pmsuryaghar.gov.in) before or immediately after installation.

## 5. Red Flags: Why Some Quotes Are Suspiciously Cheap

If a vendor quotes you ₹80,000 for a fully installed 3kW system in Tamil Nadu, be very concerned. At an authentic material cost of around ₹1,65,000 for quality components, a ₹80,000 quote is a mathematical impossibility unless corners are being cut dramatically.

**Common shortcuts used by cheap vendors:**
*   **Using B-grade or used solar cells** disguised inside panels with legitimate-looking brand stickers.
*   **Undersized inverters:** Installing a 2.5kW inverter on a 3kW panel array causes constant clipping and lost generation.
*   **Thin-gauge, non-solar-rated cables:** These degrade rapidly under UV and can cause dangerous arc faults and fires within 5 years.
*   **No earthing or lightning protection:** A completely ungrounded system is a serious safety hazard and a likely insurance void.
*   **No TNEB net meter application support:** Some vendors install the system and disappear, leaving you without a net meter and therefore unable to export surplus power or receive billing offsets.
