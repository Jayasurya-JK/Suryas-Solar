## What Is Solar Payback Period? (Simple Explanation)

The solar payback period is simply the time it takes for your solar system to "pay for itself" through electricity bill savings.

**The Formula:**
> **Payback Period = Total Investment (After Subsidy) / Annual Electricity Savings**

**Why is the payback period shrinking in Tamil Nadu?**
1.  **Subsidies:** The **PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana** significantly reduces the upfront cost.
2.  **High Tariffs:** As TNEB rates rise, your savings per unit generated increase.
3.  **Abundant Sunlight:** Tamil Nadu's excellent solar irradiation means more units generated per day, leading to faster ROI.

## Factors That Affect Solar ROI in Tamil Nadu

Several factors influence how quickly you get your money back:

1.  **Panel Technology:** **Mono PERC** and **N-Type TOPCon** panels cost slightly more but generate more power in low light, improving ROI.
2.  **Inverter Type:** High-efficiency inverters (like Growatt or Enphase) ensure less energy loss.
3.  **Rooftop Shadow:** Even a small shadow can drop generation significantly. A proper site survey is crucial.
4.  **Location:** Coastal areas like Cuddalore have high irradiation but need corrosion-resistant structures.
5.  **Tariff Category:** Higher electricity slabs mean higher savings per unit.
6.  **Installation Quality:** Poor wiring can lead to 10-15% generation loss. At **Surya's Solar**, we follow strict IEC standards.
