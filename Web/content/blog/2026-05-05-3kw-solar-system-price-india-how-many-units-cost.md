The 3kW solar system is, without question, the most popular rooftop solar installation size in India. It occupies the perfect "sweet spot" between affordability and generating capacity, and it qualifies for the maximum central government subsidy under the PM Surya Ghar scheme.

But before you commit to any purchase, you need clear, accurate, and honest answers to the most critical questions:
*   How much does a 3kW solar system actually cost in India in 2026?
*   How many units of electricity will it actually generate per day?
*   What appliances can it run?
*   How long will it take to pay for itself?

This guide answers all of those questions with real engineering data-not marketing numbers.

---

## 1. What Is a 3kW Solar System?

A 3kW (or 3 Kilowatt) solar system refers to a system with a total solar panel capacity of 3,000 Watts. In practice, this typically means:
*   **8 to 10 solar panels** of 330W to 375W each (depending on the panel wattage chosen)
*   **1 solar inverter** rated at 3kW or 3.3kW
*   **Mounting structure, cables, DCDB, ACDB, earthing, and accessories**

The system is classified as an "On-Grid" system when connected to and synchronized with the TNEB utility grid. This is the most common, cost-effective, and subsidy-eligible configuration in Tamil Nadu.

## 2. How Many Units Does a 3kW Solar System Generate Per Day?

This is the most critical performance question. The actual generation depends primarily on your geographic location and the quality of the installation.

**The Formula:**
Daily Generation = System Capacity × Peak Sun Hours × Performance Ratio
*   System Capacity: 3 kW
*   Peak Sun Hours (Tamil Nadu: Cuddalore/Puducherry): 4.5 to 5.2 hours/day
*   Performance Ratio (real-world efficiency): 0.75 to 0.80

**Calculation (using conservative values):**
*   3 × 4.5 × 0.78 = **10.53 units/day** (winter minimum)

**Calculation (using favorable summer values):**
*   3 × 5.2 × 0.80 = **12.48 units/day** (summer peak)

**Practical Answer: A well-installed 3kW system in Tamil Nadu generates approximately 11 to 14 units per day**, or approximately 330 to 420 units per month, or 3,960 to 5,040 units per year.

Over a two-month TNEB billing cycle, your 3kW system generates approximately **660 to 840 units**-comfortably covering a standard household's bi-monthly consumption.

## 3. What Can a 3kW Solar System Run?

This is perhaps the most important practical question for a homeowner. Let us be precise.

**Over a full two-month billing cycle via Net Metering (On-Grid), a 3kW system's generated units can offset the consumption of:**

*   ✅ One 1.5-ton 5-star Inverter AC running 8 to 10 hours every night
*   ✅ One double-door refrigerator running 24/7
*   ✅ 4 to 6 ceiling fans running 10 to 12 hours per day
*   ✅ LED lighting throughout the home (10 to 15 lights)
*   ✅ One washing machine (5 to 7 washes per week)
*   ✅ One TV, 3 to 4 phone chargers, laptop
*   ✅ One water pump running 1 hour per day

**Important clarifications:**
*   ❌ A 3kW system cannot reliably run **two ACs simultaneously** if you want to completely eliminate your bill. For two ACs, you need at least a 5kW system.
*   ❌ A 3kW system **cannot run appliances during TNEB power cuts** (for that, you need a Hybrid system with batteries). On-Grid inverters shut down during power cuts by law.
*   ✅ The system works by Net Metering-it generates power during the day (which you export to the grid), and your nighttime AC usage is offset by those export credits.

## 5. The PM Surya Ghar Subsidy Impact on a 3kW System

For a 3kW system, the central government's PM Surya Ghar scheme provides the maximum available subsidy:

**₹78,000 direct bank transfer** to your account within 30 to 90 days of commissioning.

This means your effective net investment for a 3kW system is:
*   Gross Installation Cost: ₹1,75,000 (average)
*   Minus Subsidy: ₹78,000
*   **Net Cost to You: ₹97,000**

For under ₹1 Lakh, you get a 25-year electricity-bill-eliminating asset on your roof. There is no bank FD or mutual fund that can guarantee this kind of financial return with zero risk.

## 7. Choosing the Right Panels for a 3kW System

Not all solar panels are created equal. For a 3kW system in Tamil Nadu's harsh coastal and hot climate, we strongly recommend:

**Monocrystalline PERC panels** (as opposed to older Polycrystalline):
*   Higher efficiency (20% to 22% vs. 15% to 18% for Poly)
*   Better performance in high-temperature conditions (lower temperature coefficient)
*   Better low-light performance in cloudy or overcast conditions
*   Eligible for PM Surya Ghar subsidy

**Recommended Indian brands** that manufacture genuine Tier-1 cells:
*   **Waaree Energies** (India's largest solar panel manufacturer)
*   **Adani Solar** (Tier-1 globally certified)
*   **Premier Energies** (Hyderabad-based, strong coastal performance track record)
*   **Tata Solar** (established brand with excellent service network in Tamil Nadu)

## 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid When Buying a 3kW System

1. **Do not buy based on price alone.** The cheapest quote almost always uses inferior components that will underperform within 5 years.
2. **Always verify MNRE empanelment.** If the vendor is not empanelled under PM Surya Ghar, you will not receive the ₹78,000 subsidy-even if you paid the full price.
3. **Insist on a written warranty document.** Verbal warranties mean nothing. Get the panel and inverter warranty in writing.
4. **Demand a site survey before finalizing.** Any legitimate vendor will do a roof measurement and shade analysis before quoting. If someone quotes you over the phone without seeing your roof, be cautious.
