If you live in Cuddalore district and you have finally decided to install a rooftop solar system, congratulations. You are making one of the smartest financial decisions available to a homeowner in Tamil Nadu in 2026. The combination of Tamil Nadu's excellent solar irradiation, high TNEB electricity tariffs, and the massive PM Surya Ghar government subsidy makes solar a genuinely compelling investment.

However, the solar installation market in Cuddalore—like most of India—is deeply unregulated. The barrier to entry is almost nonexistent. Any person with a pickup truck and access to cheap Chinese components can call themselves a "solar company" and begin offering quotes to unsuspecting homeowners. 

The result is a market full of vendors ranging from highly professional, certified engineering firms to completely unqualified fly-by-night operators who will take your money, install an inferior system, and become unreachable when it stops performing after 3 years.

This guide gives you the complete checklist of what to verify before signing any solar installation contract in Cuddalore.

---

## 1. Verify MNRE/PM Surya Ghar Empanelment (Non-Negotiable)

The single most important credential to verify is whether the solar company is **registered and empanelled under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana** by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE).

This is not optional. It is not a "nice to have." Without this empanelment:
*   You will NOT receive the ₹78,000 central government subsidy, even if you paid the full system price.
*   The installation may not be eligible for the official TNEB net meter application under the government scheme.
*   The vendor has not been verified by any government agency for quality standards.

**How to verify:** Ask the vendor directly for their PM Surya Ghar vendor registration number. You can cross-check it on the official PM Surya Ghar portal at pmsuryaghar.gov.in. Any legitimate vendor will provide this immediately and without hesitation.

---

## 2. Ask for a Physical Site Survey (Not a Phone Quote)

A legitimate solar engineering company will never give you a final system design or accurate price quote over the phone without physically visiting your property. The following factors can only be determined through an on-site assessment:

*   **Roof area and orientation:** The exact square footage of shadow-free, south-facing roof space determines the maximum system size physically possible.
*   **Structural load capacity:** Solar panels and mounting structures add significant dead weight to your roof. An engineering assessment must confirm your roof slab can handle this load safely.
*   **Shading analysis:** Trees, water tanks, parapet walls, and neighbouring buildings can cast shadows that devastate generation. A professional will use a shade analysis tool (like Solar Pathfinder or a digital app) to identify shading risks.
*   **Cable run length:** The distance from your roof to your main electrical distribution panel affects cable costs and voltage drop calculations.

**Red Flag:** If a vendor calls you on the phone and immediately quotes a fixed price without visiting your site, walk away. This vendor is either cutting corners on the engineering or intending to make up the difference later with hidden charges.

---

## 3. Demand a Component-Wise Quotation

A professional solar quotation should be fully transparent and itemized. It must clearly specify:

*   **Panel Brand and Model Number:** (e.g., "Waaree WS-335M Monocrystalline PERC") — not just "335W solar panel."
*   **Inverter Brand, Model, and Warranty Period:** (e.g., "Growatt MIN 3000TL-X, 5-year manufacturer warranty")
*   **Mounting Structure Material:** (e.g., "Hot-Dip Galvanized Steel, 3mm thickness")
*   **Cable Specification:** (e.g., "4mm² DC solar cable, UV-rated, POLYCAB")
*   **Scope of Work:** What exactly is included — does it include TNEB net meter application? Does it include TNEB liaison for the subsidy? Who handles the PM Surya Ghar portal application?

If a vendor provides only a single lump-sum quote (e.g., "3kW system fully installed for ₹1,10,000"), this is a serious red flag. You have absolutely no visibility into what quality of components you are receiving.

---

## 4. Verify the Panel and Inverter Warranties in Writing

Verbal warranties are completely worthless. Always demand the physical warranty documents before signing any agreement.

**Solar Panels:**
*   **Product warranty:** Should be a minimum of 10 to 12 years for structural integrity (frames, junction box, lamination). Tier-1 brands offer 12 to 15 year product warranties.
*   **Performance warranty:** Should be a minimum of 25 years guaranteeing the panel produces at least 80% of its rated output. This is the most critical warranty in solar.

**Solar Inverter:**
*   Standard warranty: 5 years from the manufacturer.
*   Premium brands (SMA, Fronius): 10-year extended warranties available.
*   Always check: Is the warranty serviced locally in Tamil Nadu, or does the panel have to be shipped back to the manufacturer? A long shipping warranty is nearly useless.

## 5. Assess the Structure Quality (Especially in Coastal Cuddalore)

This is a point that is almost universally overlooked by homeowners, but our engineers consider it the second most important quality factor after the panels themselves.

Cuddalore district lies directly on the Coromandel Coast. The salt-laden sea breeze that blows from the Bay of Bengal is highly corrosive to ferrous metals. A mounting structure made of standard painted mild steel (the cheapest option used by cut-rate vendors) will begin showing rust within 2 to 3 years in coastal Cuddalore areas like Semmandalam, Thambipettai, and the harbor zones.

Once the structure rusts severely, it needs to be replaced—a process that involves removing all the panels, dismantling the old structure, installing a new one, and reinstalling the panels. This can cost ₹30,000 to ₹60,000 and completely negates the savings from the first few years of solar generation.

**What to insist on:**
*   **Hot-Dip Galvanized (HDG) Steel:** This is the minimum acceptable standard for coastal areas. The steel is dipped in molten zinc, providing a sacrificial protective layer that is far superior to paint.
*   **Marine-Grade Aluminum (6061 or 6063 alloy):** The premium option for extreme coastal proximity (within 1 km of the sea). Aluminum does not rust at all.

**Always ask:** "What is the mounting structure material and the anti-corrosion treatment?" If the vendor cannot answer this question specifically, do not hire them.

## 6. Confirm the Net Meter Application Process

In Tamil Nadu, installing solar panels is only half the job. The second critical step is getting your **TNEB Bi-Directional Net Meter installed and activated.** Until this happens, you cannot export any surplus power to the grid, your existing TNEB meter is still running normally, and you are not saving anything on your bi-monthly bill.

The net meter application involves:
1.  Submitting an application to your local TNEB AEE/ADE office with the commissioning certificate from your installer.
2.  A TNEB technical inspection of your installation.
3.  TNEB removing your existing single-direction meter and replacing it with a bi-directional (import/export) smart meter.
4.  Final activation and synchronization of the net meter with your account.

This process can take anywhere from 30 to 120 days depending on your local TNEB office's workload.

**What to confirm:** Ask the vendor explicitly: "Will you handle the TNEB net meter application on our behalf? Do you have an established relationship with the Cuddalore TNEB division?" A vendor with genuine local experience in Cuddalore will have existing relationships with the relevant TNEB AEE officers and know exactly which documents are required locally.

## 7. Check for an AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract)

A quality solar system requires minimal but consistent maintenance:
*   Panel cleaning (water wash) every 4 to 8 weeks during the dusty months (March to June)
*   Annual inverter performance check
*   Periodic junction box and connector integrity inspection
*   Structure bolt torque check after cyclone season

Ask your vendor: "Do you offer a paid AMC service contract?" A reputable local solar company in Cuddalore will offer an annual maintenance package. A vendor who only appears at installation and then becomes unreachable is a significant liability.

---

## 8. Ask for Local References

The most powerful way to assess any solar company is to speak with their existing customers in Cuddalore district. Ask the vendor: "Can you give me contact details for 2 or 3 homeowners in Cuddalore where you have installed systems in the last 2 years?"

When speaking to references, ask:
*   Did the system get commissioned on time?
*   Was the TNEB net meter installed smoothly?
*   Did they receive the PM Surya Ghar subsidy in their bank account?
*   Has the vendor been responsive to any post-installation concerns?
*   Is the system generating roughly the amount the vendor originally projected?

A vendor who refuses to provide local references, or who can only provide references from distant cities, is a vendor who lacks confidence in their local track record.
