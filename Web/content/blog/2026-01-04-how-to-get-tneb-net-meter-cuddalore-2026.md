##  What is a Net Meter and Why Do You Need It?

A **Bi-Directional Net Meter** is the heart of your solar savings. Without it, your solar system is financially useless.

*   **Old Meter (Uni-directional):** It only spins forward. If you export solar power to the grid, the meter interprets it as *consumption* and charges you for it! You literally pay for the power you give to TNEB.
*   **Net Meter (Bi-directional):** It has two registers:
    *   **Import (Night):** Replaces your standard reading.
    *   **Export (Day):** Counts how many units you sent to TNEB.
    *   **Net Bill:** (Import - Export). If Export is higher, you pay **Zero**.

> **Surya's Warning:** NEVER turn on your solar inverter until the TNEB official officially replaces your meter. We have seen customers get bills of ₹10,000 because they turned the system on early!

##  The 2026 Application Roadmap: Day 1 to Day 30

In Cuddalore, the process from "Application" to "First Solar Bill" takes **21 to 30 days** on average. Here is the detailed timeline we follow for our clients.

### Stage 1: The "Feasibility" Check (Days 1-5)
The journey begins on the **TANGEDCO** or **[PM Surya Ghar](/blog/2026-01-01-pm-surya-ghar-scheme-cuddalore-2026)** portal.
*   **The Check:** Your local AE (Assistant Engineer) receives the application. They check the **Distribution Transformer (DT)** capacity on your street.
*   **The Rule:** The total solar capacity on a transformer generally shouldn't exceed 30% to 50% of the DT capacity.
*   **Cuddalore Status:** Most residential areas in Cuddalore town (Manjakuppam, Thirupapuliyur) and Neyveli still have plenty of DT capacity available in 2026. Feasibility is usually auto-approved in 48 hours.

### Stage 2: Safety Letter & Installation (Days 6-15)
Once feasibility is approved, TNEB issues a "Safety Letter". This is your green light to install the solar plant.
*   **Our Job:** Surya's Solar installs panels, inverters, and crucially, the **[Earthing](/blog/2026-01-13-solar-earthing-safety-guide) Pits**.
*   **The Inspection Trap:** TNEB requires strict earthing values.
    *   **DC Earthing:** For panels (Lightning protection).
    *   **AC Earthing:** For inverter (Grid safety).
    *   **Lightning Arrester:** Mandatory for buildings above 10m height.
*   **The Test:** The earth resistance must be **less than 5 Ohms**. If we install a weak earth rod, the AE will reject the file instantly.

### Stage 3: The Work Completion Report (Days 16-20)
We upload the "Work Completion Report" with photos of your plant, the earthing pits, and the single-line diagram (SLD). This triggers the **Inspection Request**.

**The Inspection Day Checklist:**
When the AE visits your home, they check:
1.  **SPDs (Surge Protection Devices):** Are they visible in the distribution boxes?
2.  **Cable Safety:** Are [cables](/blog/2026-01-14-solar-dc-cable-quality-guide) routed through conduits/pipes? (Loose hanging wires are a rejection).
3.  **Manual Disconnect Switch:** Is there a visible switch near the meter to cut off solar power? (Essential for lineman safety during repairs).
4.  **DCR Labels:** They may scan panel barcodes to verify "Made in India" status for subsidies.

### Stage 4: Meter Swap & Commissioning (Days 21-30)
*   **Programming:** The new Net Meter comes from the TNEB lab (MRT division), pre-programmed with your specific consumer tariff.
*   **Physical Swap:** The TNEB wireman removes your old meter and seals the new one.
*   **Asset Tagging:** They take a photo of the "Solar Plant ID" sticker.

**CONGRATULATIONS! Your plant is effectively live.**

## Warning: "Why is my Application Stuck?" (Troubleshooting Guide)

If your status is pending for >15 days, it is usually one of these 3 reasons. We check these *before* applying:

### 1. The Name Mismatch Nightmare
*   **Issue:** Your Property Tax receipt says "R. Senthil" but the TNEB Card says "P. Senthil Kumar" (Father's name vs Initials).
*   **Result:** Application Rejected.
*   **Fix:** We advise applying for a "Name Transfer" in TNEB first (costs ₹600) to match your ID proof exactly.

### 2. The "Rusted Box" Complaint
*   **Issue:** Your existing service wire or meter box is 20 years old, rusted, or broken.
*   **Result:** The AE will refuse to install a new expensive Net Meter in a broken box.
*   **Fix:** You must replace the Meter Box (Board) before inspection. We can arrange an electrician to do this for ₹1,500.

### 3. The "High Tension" Hazard
*   **Issue:** Your solar cables run dangerously close to other high-tension wires or telephone lines.
*   **Result:** Safety Hazard Notice.
*   **Fix:** We adhere to strict IEC standards for cable routing, keeping a safe distance from other utilities.
