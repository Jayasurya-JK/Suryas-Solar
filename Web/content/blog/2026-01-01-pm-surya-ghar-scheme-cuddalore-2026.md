## The Core Promise: What is PM Surya Ghar 2026?

The "Muft Bijli Yojana" (Free Electricity Scheme) aims to power 1 crore households across India with rooftop solar. The government’s goal is simple: **reduce the burden on the national grid** and **lower electricity bills for the common man**.

In 2026, the scheme has been streamlined. It is no longer just about "getting solar"—it is about becoming an independent power producer.

### Key Highlights for 2026:
1.  **Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT):** The subsidy is NOT given to the vendor; it is credited directly to **your** bank account within 30 days of commissioning.
2.  **Increased Cap:** The subsidy applies effectively up to 3kW capacity, covering the bulk of residential system costs.
3.  **Digital First:** The entire process, from application to feasibility to inspection requests, is handled via the National Portal.

## Yes Are You Eligible? (The Checklist)

Before you upload documents, run through this checklist. TNEB Cuddalore is strict about these parameters, and deviations can lead to rejection.

### 1. Consumer Connection Status
You must be a residential consumer with an active TANGEDCO (TNEB) connection.
*   **Eligible:** Tariff 1A (Domestic).
*   **Not Eligible:** Commercial, Industrial, or Temporary tariff connections.

### 2. Sanctioned Load
Your solar system capacity should generally align with your sanctioned load.
*   *Scenario:* Your TNEB card says "1kW Sanctioned Load", but you want a 3kW Solar Plant.
*   *Solution:* You must apply for a **Load Enhancement** at your local EB office *before* or *during* the solar application. We handle this paperwork for our clients.

### 3. Roof Ownership & Structure
You must have roof rights.
*   **Concrete Roof:** Ideal.
*   **Sheet Roof:** Requires specialized aluminium rails (short rails) or penetrating structures.
*   **Apartments:** You need a NOC (No Objection Certificate) from the association if using a common roof.

### 4. The "DCR" Mandate
This is critical. To get the subsidy, your solar panels **MUST** be "Made in India" using Indian solar cells. These are called **DCR (Domestic Content Requirement)** modules.
*   If you install imported components (even high-end foreign brands), you forfeit the subsidy.
*   At **Surya's Solar**, we only stock ALMM-approved DCR modules (like Tata Power, Adani, Wikree, Premier) for residential projects to guarantee your eligibility.

## The Documentation Packet

In 2026, the portal rejects blurry or outdated documents instantly using AI checks. Ensure you have clear PDFs/JPGs of:

1.  **Recent Electricity Bill:** Must be from the last 2 months. The Consumer Number and Name must be clearly visible.
2.  **Aadhar Card:** Both sides. The address isn't critical, but the ID proof is.
3.  **Bank Passbook or Cancelled Cheque:** This is where the money goes. The name on the bank account **MUST MATCH** the name on the electricity bill. *This is the #1 reason for delay.*
4.  **Property Tax Receipt:** Sometimes requested by TNEB Engineers in Cuddalore to verify premises ownership.

## Step-by-Step Application Process (The Cuddalore Guide)

We believe in transparency. While we do this work for our clients, it is empowering for you to know the process.

### Phase 1: The Digital Application
1.  **Register:** Visit [pmsuryaghar.gov.in](https://pmsuryaghar.gov.in). Select "State: Tamil Nadu" and "District: Cuddalore". Enter your Consumer Number.
2.  **Mobile OTP:** Register with the mobile number linked to your TNEB account.
3.  **Apply:** Fill in the basic details (Sanctioned Load, Solar Capacity). Upload the Electricity Bill.
4.  **Feasibility Approval:** The application goes to the local TNEB Assistant Engineer (AE). They check if the local distribution transformer can handle your solar power. in 2026, this approval is usually auto-generated or cleared within 3-7 days.

### Phase 2: Installation
1.  **Choose Vendor:** Once approved, you select a vendor. Search for **"Surya's Solar"** or your preferred certified installer.
2.  **Agreement:** We sign a model agreement with you (a portal requirement).
3.  **Installation:** Our team arrives. We mount the structure, fix the DCR panels, wire the DCDB/ACDB boxes, and earth the system (Chemical [Earthing](/blog/2026-01-13-solar-earthing-safety-guide)).
4.  **Submission:** We upload photos of the installed plant, the earthing pits, and the "Solar Asset" tag to the portal.

### Phase 3: The [Net Meter](/blog/2026-01-04-how-to-get-tneb-net-meter-cuddalore-2026) Inspection
This is the most crucial physical step.
1.  We submit the "Work Completion Report" online.
2.  You pay the **Net Meter charges** to TNEB (approx ₹1,500 - ₹3,000 depending on single/three-phase).
3.  The **TNEB AE and Tester** visit your home. They check:
    *   Is the earthing value below 5 Ohms?
    *   Is the inverter working efficiently?
    *   Are the safety breakers (MCBs/SPDs) installed?
4.  If satisfied, they replace your old meter with a **Bi-Directional Net Meter**.

### Phase 4: Subsidy Release
1.  TNEB issues a "Commissioning Certificate" online.
2.  You log in one last time and enter your Bank Account details and upload a Cancelled Cheque.
3.  **Wait 15-30 Days.** The Central Government processes the batch, and the funds hit your account via DBT.

## Warning: Common Pitfalls & Rejection Reasons

Why do some neighbors complain about "pending subsidies"? Usually, it's a paperwork error.

*   **Name Mismatch:** If [TNEB Bill](/blog/2026-01-17-understanding-tneb-solar-bill) says "R. Senthil" and Bank Account says "Senthil Kumar R", the system might flag it. We advise creating a joint account or updating the TNEB name if strict matching is required.
*   **Shadow Casting:** If a tree or neighbor's building casts a shadow on panels during the TNEB inspection, they may deny commissioning until panels are relocated.
*   **Non-DCR Panels:** Some dishonest vendors install cheaper imported panels to increase their margin. The portal requires scanning the barcode of every panel. If it's not DCR, the system blocks the subsidy instantly. **Always verify the DCR label on your panels.**

##  What About "Free Electricity"? How Does Net Metering Work?

The subsidy is a one-time cash benefit. The real "Free Electricity" comes from the **Net Metering** mechanism.

*   **Daytime:** Your panels generate power (e.g., 15 units). Your house uses 5 units. The extra 10 units go to the TNEB grid.
*   **Nighttime:** Solar stops. Your house pulls power from TNEB.
*   **The Calculation:** At the end of the billing cycle (2 months), TNEB calculates: `(Exported Units - Imported Units)`.
    *   If you Exported more: You pay **₹0** (plus fixed charges). The balance units are carried forward (Banked).
    *   If you Imported more: You only pay for the net difference.
