## Types of Solar Panels (2025 Overview)

Before looking at brands, it's crucial to understand the technology. The "best" brand for you depends on which technology fits your budget and roof space.

### a) Mono PERC (Most Common for Homes)
**Monocrystalline Passivated Emitter and Rear Cell (PERC)** panels are currently the gold standard for residential rooftops in Tamil Nadu.
*   **Why it works:** They offer a perfect balance of price and performance.
*   **Efficiency:** Typically 20% ‚Äì 21.5%.
*   **Best For:** Most homes in **Cuddalore and Panruti** with standard roof space.

### b) N-Type TOPCon (Premium, Next-Gen)
**Tunnel Oxide Passivated Contact (TOPCon)** is the latest buzzword in the solar industry.
*   **Why it works:** These panels have lower degradation rates and perform better in high temperatures‚Äîa huge plus for the hot summers in **Neyveli and Chidambaram**.
*   **Efficiency:** Can exceed 22.5%.
*   **Best For:** Homeowners who want the absolute highest generation and have slightly higher budgets.

### c) Polycrystalline (Budget, Outdated)
These are the older, blue-colored panels.
*   **Why avoid:** They are less efficient and require more roof space.
*   **Verdict:** We generally **do not recommend** these for new residential installations in 2025 as the price gap with Mono PERC has narrowed significantly.

## Which Panel Is Best for Homes in Tamil Nadu & Puducherry?

Our region presents unique challenges and opportunities for solar.
*   **Tamil Nadu** receives 300+ sunny days a year.
*   **Puducherry** has a humid, coastal climate.

**Mono PERC & TOPCon** are the best choices here. You should avoid polycrystalline panels for new installations.

### Local Considerations for Choosing a Brand

**1. Coastal Areas (Cuddalore, Puducherry)**
*   **Challenge:** Salt mist corrosion and high humidity.
*   **Recommendation:** Choose brands like **Waaree or Tata** that have rigorous salt-mist corrosion certification (IEC 61701).
*   **Tip:** Ensure your installer uses Hot-Dip Galvanized structures to match the panel's longevity.

**2. High-Heat Areas (Neyveli, Panruti)**
*   **Challenge:** Solar panels actually lose efficiency as they get hotter (above 25¬∞C).
*   **Recommendation:** **Adani TOPCon or Panasonic**. These panels have a better "temperature coefficient," meaning they lose less power when the sun is blazing hot.

**3. Mixed Weather (Chidambaram)**
*   **Challenge:** Variable cloud cover during monsoon.
*   **Recommendation:** **Mono PERC** panels from **Premier or Loom Solar** offer excellent low-light performance, keeping your generation steady even on cloudy days.
